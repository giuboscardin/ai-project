🧭A Simple 4-Step Framework for AI at Newfold

This framework helps you think through your AI needs so that when you submit a request, we can move quickly and say yes with confidence.
1

Identify Your Use Case

Start with your day-to-day work. Ask yourself:

  • What tasks take a long time or feel overly manual?
  • What work is repetitive or could be done more efficiently?
  • Where am I spending time that could be better used on higher-value work?

Common examples: drafting or summarizing content, analyzing data, researching topics, or reducing recurring admin work. The key is identifying a real problem.

2

Align with Your Manager

Share your idea with your manager and team:

  • Is this problem worth solving?
  • Does it align with team or business priorities?
  • Is there clear value if this work becomes faster or easier?
3

Research and Experiment

Once you and your manager agree it's worth pursuing:

  • Use Copilot Chat (Teams or Edge) or Nova to explore AI approaches.
  • Try tools through personal or free versions to get hands-on experience.
  • Identify which tools best fit your use case and why.
Important: Do not upload company-sensitive information into non-corporate tools.

You don't need all the answers — understanding the problem matters more than naming the perfect tool. IT will help with the rest.

4

Submit an IT Request

When you've done the thinking and research, submit a request through the ServiceNow IT Portal. Include:

  • Use Case Description — the problem you're trying to solve.
  • Estimated Impact — time or cost savings, even as a rough estimate.
  • Tools Identified — what you researched and why it fits.
📋 Submit AI Tools Request →

IT will review your request and work with you to find the best solution. Our goal is to ensure every paid AI tool has a clear purpose — so we can invest wisely and keep expanding access.

❓Quick FAQs

Common questions about getting started with AI at Newfold.
Do I need to know exactly which AI tool I want before submitting a request?
No. What matters most is that you can clearly describe the problem you're trying to solve. If you're not sure which tool is the best fit, that's perfectly fine — do some basic research, share what you found, and IT will help recommend the right solution.
What does a strong AI use case look like?
Strong use cases focus on saving time, reducing manual effort, or improving quality. For example: drafting or rewriting recurring reports, analyzing or cleaning data in Excel, researching and synthesizing large amounts of content, or reducing repetitive admin work. If you can describe what's painful today and how AI could help, you're on the right track.
What if my idea isn't fully formed yet?
That's completely normal. Start by experimenting in Copilot Chat or Nova — that's exactly what they're there for. As you explore, your use case will sharpen. The key is to have a clear problem in mind before requesting a paid tool, so IT can match you with the right solution. If you're stuck, reach out — we're happy to help you think it through.
Am I allowed to experiment with AI?
Absolutely. Microsoft Copilot Chat and Nova are available to every employee at no cost, and they're the best place to experiment, learn, and develop your ideas. We encourage exploration — the free tools are there specifically for that purpose.
What if I can't clearly quantify the ROI?
That's okay. We're not asking for a formal business case. Even a rough estimate like "this could save me an hour a week" or "this reduces manual rework on a recurring task" is helpful. A thoughtful estimate is always better than no estimate at all.
Why do some people already have premium AI tools?
Some teams and roles had approved use cases prior to this process being introduced. Going forward, this framework standardizes how AI tools are requested and approved across the company — making it consistent and fair for everyone. If you have a strong use case, this process is designed to get you what you need.
What's the fastest way to get started today?
Open Copilot Chat (in Teams, Edge or MyApps portal) and start experimenting with your everyday work. When you've identified a clear, high-value use case, submit a request via the 📋 AI Tools Request Form (ServiceNow).
